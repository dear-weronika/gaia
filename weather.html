Weather

Our daily weather and normal temperatures will change as climate change takes place all over the world hundreds of plants and animals will be affected. Many crops grown around the world will also be effected by changes in weather. Low lying coastal areas such as the Netherlands and Bangladesh will be at great risk as increasing temperatures caused by climate change will make the water expand. Countries whose coastal regions have a large population may have to move inland to avoid flooding. In many regions all across the world there is a major demand for water. Climate change causes disruption to weather patterns which means some countries will experience more rainfall than others. Meaning dry areas will become dryer causing people to suffer starvation and dehydration and wet areas will become wetter causing flooding and homes and land to be destroyed.

Carbon Brief’s analysis suggests 68% of all extreme weather events studied to date were made more likely or more severe by human-caused climate change. Heatwaves account for 43% such events, droughts make up 17% and heavy rainfall or floods account for 16%.
